Where to Watch

6 Key Birding Locations · Cyprus

Cyprus sits at a major migration crossroads between Europe, Asia and Africa. These six sites cover the full spectrum — from salt lakes with flamingos and the world's best site for Demoiselle Cranes, to mountain forests with all endemic species.

🏖️
Akrotiri Peninsula · Limassol
Akrotiri Salt Lake & Marsh
🗺 Loop 4–6 km ⭐ Best site on island 📅 Year-round
The number-one birding destination in Cyprus. The salt lake holds up to 12,000 Greater Flamingos in winter. Lady's Mile beach is a spring migration trap. The reedbeds and marsh host breeding Spur-winged Lapwings and rare ducks. Late August brings thousands of Demoiselle Cranes — the best site in the Western Palearctic. Raptors funnel through Akrotiri cliffs year-round.
Key species
Greater Flamingo Demoiselle Crane Eleonora's Falcon Spur-winged Lapwing Griffon Vulture Black-winged Stilt
eBird Hotspot →
🦩
Larnaca · Salt Lake
Larnaca Salt Lake Circuit
🗺 Loop 3 km 🟢 Easy walk 📅 Nov – Mar best
One of the largest natural salt lakes in the Mediterranean. World-famous for wintering flamingos — up to 8,000 birds visible from the airport road. Natura 2000 site. The mosque on the western shore offers elevated views over the entire lake. Waders and ducks concentrate in shallow margins. Kentish Plovers breed on the salt flats.
Key species
Greater Flamingo Kentish Plover Little Stint Black-winged Stilt Eurasian Teal
eBird Hotspot →
🌲
Troodos Mountains · Nicosia–Limassol road
Caledonia & Artemis Trails
🗺 2–6 km options 🟡 Moderate 📅 Year-round
The only place to reliably find all four endemic species in one visit. The Caledonia Trail drops through pine forest to a waterfall — Cyprus Warblers sing from the scrub, Cyprus Scops Owls call at dusk. At higher elevations the Artemis Trail circles Mt Olympus: Cyprus Jays in the cedar zone, Bonelli's Eagles overhead. Spring mornings are magical.
Key species
Cyprus Warbler Cyprus Jay Cyprus Scops Owl Cyprus Wheatear Bonelli's Eagle Short-toed Snake Eagle

🌊
Cape Greco · Famagusta Bay
Cape Greco Headland
🗺 2–4 km 🟢 Easy 📅 Aug – Nov best
A narrow limestone headland that concentrates migrating passerines in autumn. The sea-watch from the point produces seabirds, skuas and terns. Warblers, flycatchers and shrikes pile into the low scrub. In spring, Bee-eaters and Rollers cruise overhead. Pallid Swifts breed in the sea caves below.
Key species
Collared Flycatcher Garden Warbler Honey Buzzard Red-footed Falcon Black Stork Pallid Swift
eBird Hotspot →
🌿
Oroklini · Larnaca District
Oroklini Lake
🗺 Loop 2 km 🟢 Easy 📅 Year-round
One of only seven natural wetlands in Cyprus and a Special Protection Area. A shallow seasonal lake with reedbeds and muddy margins. Excellent for waders on passage. Breeding Spur-winged Lapwings are noisy in spring. Purple Herons hunt in the reed edges. Cetti's Warblers explode from cover without warning.
Key species
Purple Heron Cetti's Warbler Common Kingfisher Spur-winged Lapwing Common Sandpiper
eBird Hotspot →
💧
Paralimni · Famagusta District
Paralimni Lake
🗺 1–2 km 🟢 Easy 📅 Oct – Apr best
A seasonal freshwater lake in the northeast. Water levels peak in winter, attracting large duck flocks and occasional flamingos. 186 species recorded here. Surrounded by citrus groves and scrub that hold winter passerines. Corn Buntings gather in post-harvest stubble fields nearby. The eastern location catches vagrants from the Middle East.
Key species
Greater Flamingo Eurasian Teal Corn Bunting Eurasian Wigeon Common Crane
